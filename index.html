Wait, what is "DNS Verification"?

Think of the Internet as a giant smartphone. DNS (Domain Name System) is the Contacts List (Phonebook).

When someone types your website name (e.g., yourbusiness.com), the DNS tells the computer which number to dial (IP Address) to find your website. DNS Verification is simply checking that Contacts List to make sure the numbers are correct, so customers don't get a "Number Disconnected" error or your emails don't go to Spam.

The Virtual Assistant's Action Plan

Here is the exact list of duties your VA will perform, translated from "Geek Speak" to "Plain English."

🔎

1. The "Is It Alive?" Check

Technical Term: A-Record Verification

What they do: Simply type your website into a browser (and a tool like "Whatsmydns") to make sure it actually loads for people around the world.

📧

2. The Email Safety Check

Technical Term: MX & TXT Records

What they do: Ensure your email isn't going to Spam. They check special "notes" in the system (SPF/DKIM) that tell Gmail and Outlook "Yes, this email is really from us."

🔒

3. The Security Padlock Check

Technical Term: SSL/CNAME Verification

What they do: Verify that the little padlock icon appears next to your website URL. This proves your site is secure for visitors.

📝

4. The "Update & Report"

Technical Term: Propagation Monitoring

What they do: If a change is made, they watch it to ensure the change updates everywhere (like waiting for a Facebook post to publish), then send you a Green Thumbs Up.
